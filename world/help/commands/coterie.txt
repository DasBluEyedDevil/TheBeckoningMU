|W║                      COTERIE COMMANDS                             |
|[R╔═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗|n

Create and manage vampire coteries (groups and alliances).

|WCOMMANDS:|n

  |y+coterie|n                         - View your coterie information
  |y+coterie/create <name>|n           - Create a new coterie
  |y+coterie/invite <character>|n      - Invite someone to your coterie
  |y+coterie/accept <coterie>|n        - Accept coterie invitation
  |y+coterie/leave|n                   - Leave your current coterie
  |y+coterie/promote <character>|n     - Promote member to lieutenant
  |y+coterie/demote <character>|n      - Demote lieutenant to member
  |y+coterie/kick <character>|n        - Remove member from coterie
  |y+coterie/resource <type>=<dots>|n  - Allocate coterie resources
  |y+social|n                          - View social connections and standing

|WEXAMPLES:|n

  |y+coterie|n
    View your current coterie, members, and resources

  |y+coterie/create Night Watch|n
    Create a coterie named "Night Watch"

  |y+coterie/invite Bob|n
    Invite Bob to join your coterie

  |y+coterie/accept Night Watch|n
    Accept invitation to join "Night Watch" coterie

  |y+coterie/promote Alice|n
    Promote Alice to lieutenant role

  |y+coterie/resource domain=3|n
    Allocate 3 dots to coterie Domain resource

  |y+social|n
    View all your social connections, allies, and relationships

|WCOTERIE BENEFITS:|n

Coteries provide:
  - |yStrength in numbers|n - pooled resources and support
  - |yShared resources|n - Domain, Haven, Herd, Contacts
  - |yPolitical power|n - unified voice in Camarilla
  - |ySocial standing|n - reputation as a group
  - |ySafety|n - mutual protection and backup

|WCOTERIE ROLES:|n

  |yLeader|n - Founder and primary decision-maker
    - Can invite/kick members
    - Manages coterie resources
    - Represents coterie politically

  |yLieutenant|n - Trusted second-in-command
    - Can invite members
    - Assists with management
    - Deputizes for leader

  |yMember|n - Regular coterie participant
    - Benefits from coterie resources
    - Contributes to coterie goals
    - Has voice in decisions

|WCOTERIE RESOURCES:|n

Coteries can pool background dots:

  |yDomain|n - Controlled territory in the city
    - Hunting grounds
    - Influence over area
    - Protection from rivals

  |yHaven|n - Shared secure location
    - Size and security based on dots
    - Safe meeting place
    - Storage and facilities

  |yHerd|n - Shared feeding pool
    - Regular mortal vessels
    - Reduces hunting risk
    - Provides steady blood supply

  |yContacts|n - Shared information network
    - Mortal connections
    - Access to information
    - Social and practical benefits

  |yRetainers|n - Shared ghouls or servants
    - Loyal servants
    - Daytime activities
    - Specialized skills

|WCOTERIE CREATION:|n

When creating a coterie:
1. Choose a meaningful name
2. Define coterie purpose/concept
3. Invite trusted allies
4. Pool resources for mutual benefit
5. Establish hierarchy and roles

|WCOTERIE POLITICS:|n

  - Coteries are the |ybasic unit|n of Camarilla politics
  - Prince recognizes and tracks coteries
  - Coteries compete for domain and influence
  - Internal disputes should be resolved privately
  - Betraying your coterie is a serious offense

|WCOTERIE TYPES:|n

Common coterie concepts:
  - |ySecurity:|n Sheriff's enforcers
  - |yInvestigation:|n Mystery solvers
  - |yPolitical:|n Social climbers and schemers
  - |yMercenary:|n Muscle for hire
  - |yCriminal:|n Organized crime operators
  - |yCultural:|n Artists, performers, socialites

|WNOTES:|n

  - Choose coterie-mates carefully - trust is essential
  - Coterie resources belong to the group, not individuals
  - Internal conflict weakens the entire coterie
  - Coteries can be dissolved if all members leave
  - Staff mediates serious coterie disputes
