Skills represent learned abilities and training. Characters have 27 skills
rated from 0 (untrained) to 5 (master).

|WPHYSICAL SKILLS:|n

|yAthletics|n - Running, jumping, climbing, swimming
  - Defense pool (Dexterity + Athletics)
  - Chasing and fleeing
  - Parkour and acrobatics

|yBrawl|n - Unarmed combat and grappling
  - Fistfights and wrestling
  - Martial arts
  - Typically paired with Strength

|yCraft|n - Creating and repairing physical objects
  - Carpentry, mechanics, electronics
  - Art and sculpture
  - Sabotage and demolition

|yDrive|n - Operating vehicles
  - Cars, motorcycles, trucks
  - Chases and evasive driving
  - Vehicle maintenance

|yFirearms|n - Shooting guns
  - Pistols, rifles, shotguns
  - Accuracy and weapon maintenance
  - Tactical shooting

|yLarceny|n - Security systems and locks
  - Lockpicking
  - Bypass alarms
  - Sabotage security

|yMelee|n - Armed combat with hand weapons
  - Swords, knives, clubs
  - Improvised weapons
  - Parrying and defense

|yStealth|n - Moving unseen and unheard
  - Sneaking and hiding
  - Tailing targets
  - Ambushes

|ySurvival|n - Wilderness skills and tracking
  - Finding shelter and food
  - Tracking prey or enemies
  - Navigation
  - Understanding nature

|WCHARACTER CREATION:|n

During chargen, distribute skills in priority:
  - |yPrimary:|n 13 dots
  - |ySecondary:|n 9 dots
  - |yTertiary:|n 5 dots

Skills start at |w0|n (untrained) by default.
Maximum during chargen: |w3|n
Maximum with XP: |w5|n

|WSKILL RATINGS:|n

  |y0|n - Untrained: No formal training
  |y1|n - Novice: Basic competence
  |y2|n - Practiced: Professional level
  |y3|n - Expert: Among the best
  |y4|n - Master: World-class ability
  |y5|n - Legend: Peak human achievement

|WXP COSTS:|n

Raising skills costs: |yCurrent Rating × 3 XP|n

Examples:
  - Brawl 0→1: 0 XP (first dot free) or 3 XP
  - Stealth 1→2: 3 XP (1 × 3)
  - Persuasion 2→3: 6 XP (2 × 3)

|WSPECIALTIES:|n

At Skill 2+, you can purchase |ySpecialties|n (3 XP each):
  - Narrow focus within a skill
  - +1 die when specialty applies
  - Examples:
    * Brawl (Grappling)
    * Firearms (Pistols)
    * Persuasion (Seduction)
    * Academics (History)

|WTIPS FOR ALLOCATION:|n

  - Choose skills that match your concept
  - Don't spread too thin - focus is better
  - Consider synergies with disciplines
  - Every character needs some combat ability
  - Social skills matter in Camarilla politics
  - Zero dots means you can still attempt (but poorly)

|WNOTES:|n

  - Skills are cheaper than Attributes (good for XP spending)
  - Zero dots is valid - you're just untrained
  - Specialties add flavor and mechanical benefit
  - Some tasks may require minimum skill rating
